How we fight together.

When the Sunfire Castle battle starts, text chat isn't fast enough. We run live Discord voice during every castle battle. Here's how it works and what role you might play.

>_Why voice

Castle battles are decided in seconds. A rally that lands a second late, a swap that nobody called, a counter-rally that opens too early — these are the differences between winning and losing the castle. Voice coordination closes that gap. When it's working well, it feels like a machine. When it isn't, it shows.

We've put in the work to make it feel like the former.

>_The roles

Everyone on voice has a job. Here's how it breaks down:

Decision

Rally / Counter-Rally Leaders

The decision-makers during the battle. They time rallies at the castle, coordinate with each other to hit perfect windows, and call counter-rallies the moment the enemy hits. Goal: keep our state in the castle and minimize the opponent's time there.

Execution

Rally Managers

The rally fillers — making sure every march going to the castle has the right troops in the right order. We fill by priority: Helios first, then FC5 down to FC1. FC4 and FC5 troops take precedence for castle rallies. At the 5-second swap window, the garrison needs to be clear — Rally Managers and Rally Leaders coordinate this timing carefully.

Comms

Comms Team

The bridge between Discord voice and alliance chat. Not everyone is on voice — the Comms Team makes sure calls reach everyone in real time, in both directions. They relay what the leaders say and flag what's happening in chat.

Intel

Overwatch

Eyes on the enemy. Overwatch is on voice throughout, watching for enemy rally launches, counting down counter-rally windows, and reading the opponent's strategy. When something unexpected happens, Overwatch calls it first.

>_Battle etiquette

Two simple rules that make voice ops run smoothly:

01
Join rallies after the first minute. Designated joiners go first — this ensures the right hero composition before the rally fills. We're stat maxing.
02
Don't complain if you're removed from a rally. Rally Leaders and Managers are optimizing hero loadouts in real time. It's not personal.
>_Language
We primarily run voice in English, but 3156 isn't limited by language. We've figured out ways to communicate with non-native speakers — you just have to stay alert. If you're worried about the language barrier, don't be. Show up and we'll make it work.
>_Want to get involved?

Voice ops is open to anyone who wants to contribute beyond just filling rallies. If you're interested in taking a role — whether that's Overwatch, Comms, or working toward a Rally Leader spot — talk to your R4 or R5. We develop people. That's the point.
