// What happened

April 2026. We went into the cycle with nine straight prep wins and hit 6.2B in prep — the highest score we'd ever posted. We executed the playbook. We showed up. We ran the system.

We still lost both phases. Not because we played badly. Because we were matched against the series #1 state — the highest-ranked state in Group 14 — who outscored us in prep and took the Sunfire Castle in the battle phase.

// What it means

There's a version of this postmortem that blames the matchmaking, or claims a moral victory because of the score. We're not writing that version.

The data was clear before the battle started: they had more power, more depth, more whales. We closed the gap with coordination and prep discipline — 6.2B is real — but we couldn't close it entirely. That's not a failure of the system. That's the system working correctly and hitting its ceiling against a stronger opponent on the day.

"Outcomes over ego. Every time."

// What we did next

We ran the numbers, identified the gaps, and came back in May with a 5.1B prep win and a battle win. Not because we forgot April — because we used it. The postmortem became the prep plan for the next cycle.

That's the loop. That's the whole pitch. If you want to be part of a state that wins clean, loses clean, and shows up the next month — the door is open.
